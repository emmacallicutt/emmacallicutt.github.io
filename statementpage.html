My work reflects my interest in life’s unfairness coupled with the nature of its resiliency. Like many, I am a young person acquainted with sentiments of hopelessness and discouragement about my future. For me, the creation of art functions as a response to these circumstances. It is a way for me to strive towards healing and deliverance through embracing what is painful.

What drives me to make art is curiosity about the force that challenges us to make an effort amidst strife, uncertainty, and the existential threat of futility. My drawings document the beginnings of what I believe will become a lifelong path of developing a visual language that can speak on this mysterious incentive. By forming a visual tension between the primordial and futuristic, terrifying and beautiful, painterly and illustrative, I’m striving to find evidence of this force within my imagery. I have come to see the creation of art, in and of itself, as a protest against the temptation to relinquish hope.
